A Simple Pork Souvlaki Recipe

Description

Pork souvlaki is a beloved Greek street food: marinated pork skewers grilled until charred and juicy, then served with warm pita, lemon, and creamy tzatziki. It’s weeknight‑friendly, cookout‑ready, and seriously delicious.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) pork shoulder or loin, cut into 1-inch (2.5cm) cubes
  • 3 tablespoons (45ml) extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) fresh lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add pork and toss to coat.
  2. Cover and marinate at least 30 minutes (or up to overnight in the refrigerator) for best flavor./li>
  3. Thread pork onto metal or soaked wooden skewers. Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill 8 to 10 minutes, turning every couple of minutes, until nicely charred and the center reaches 145°F (63°C).
  5. Transfer to a plate, tent loosely with foil, and rest 5 minutes. Squeeze with extra lemon.
  6. Serve with warm pita, sliced tomatoes and onion, and tzatziki. Leftovers keep 3 days in the fridge.
